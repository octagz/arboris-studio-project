# Product Context: Cloud-Native Analytics Platform

## Product Goals
- Launch a cloud-native analytics platform to compete with Tableau and Power BI
- Target initial launch within 12 months
- Achieve $10M ARR within 24 months post-launch
- Establish market presence in enterprise data visualization space

## Target Markets
### Primary Market (Year 1)
- Enterprise companies with 500-5000 employees
- Mid-market SaaS companies
- Estimated TAM: $8 billion
- Estimated SAM: $2 billion (accessible market)
- Estimated SOM: $200 million (realistic market share target)

### Secondary Market (Year 2-3)
- Large enterprises (5000+ employees)
- Government agencies
- Fortune 1000 companies

## Key Features & Capabilities
### MVP Features
1. Drag-and-drop dashboard builder
2. Real-time data connectors (50+ sources)
3. Collaborative sharing and permissions
4. Mobile-responsive design
5. Basic AI-powered insights

### Differentiators
- 5x faster query performance vs competitors
- AI-assisted natural language querying
- No-code/low-code customization
- Superior mobile experience

## Financial Projections
### Investment Required
- Development team: $2.5M annually
- Infrastructure: $800K annually
- Marketing & Sales: $1.5M annually
- Operations: $300K annually
- Total Year 1: $5.1M

### Revenue Projections
- Year 1: $500K ARR (pilot customers)
- Year 2: $5M ARR (mid-market focus)
- Year 3: $12M ARR (enterprise expansion)

### Unit Economics
- Average contract value: $50K annually
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC): $25K
- Gross margin: 75%
- LTV:CAC ratio target: 3:1

## Technical Requirements
- Cloud-native architecture (AWS/GCP)
- Modern tech stack: React, Python, PostgreSQL
- Microservices architecture
- API-first design
- Security compliance: SOC 2, GDPR
- 99.9% uptime SLA requirement

## Technical Constraints
- Limited engineering team (10 engineers initially)
- No prior data visualization platform experience
- Must integrate with existing customer data ecosystems
- Need to build vs. buy for core analytics engine

## Competitive Landscape
### Major Competitors
1. Tableau (Salesforce) - Market leader, established
2. Power BI (Microsoft) - Strong enterprise traction
3. Looker (Google) - Tech-forward, good UX
4. Qlik Sense - Established, complex

### Competitive Positioning
- Strength: Performance and AI capabilities
- Weakness: Brand recognition and ecosystem
- Opportunity: Mid-market underserved by incumbents
- Threat: Competitors can undercut on pricing

## Team & Resources
- Engineering: 10 developers (2 backends, 3 frontend, 2 data, 3 full-stack)
- Product: 2 PMs
- Design: 1 designer
- Marketing: 1 marketing manager (Year 1)
- Sales: 2 AEs (Year 2)

## Market Risks & Challenges
- Customer acquisition in crowded market
- Data security and compliance requirements
- Need to prove value quickly vs established players
- Dependency on data connector ecosystem
- Potential pivot to different vertical if initial traction low
